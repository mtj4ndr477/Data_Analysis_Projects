Project Overview

This notebook details the project teams approach to building a risk prediction model for credit card customers. By leveraging numerical features, statistical checks, and predictive modeling, the team aims to forecast which customers require attention, supporting proactive risk management strategies.

Objectives:

  • Select and preprocess relevant features
  • Address multicollinearity (correlation, VIF)
  • Handle class imbalance issues
  • Train and evaluate predictive models
  • Validate results with cross-validation
  • Provide recommendations for operational use

To further address multicollinearity, we calculated the Variable Inflation Factor (VIF) for each numerical feature. Features with high VIF values were considered redundant and excluded from the predictive model.

Imbalance Ratio: 2.71

The dataset exhibits an imbalance ratio of approximately 2.7:1 between negative and positive cases. This could challenge the models ability to capture patterns in the minority class. As a mitigation strategy, the team considered resampling techniques (oversampling/undersampling) if predictive performance on unseen data proves inadequate.
